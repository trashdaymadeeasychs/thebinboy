# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Busy suburban homeowners in the Charleston / Summerville SC area. They found The Bin Boy via Google search or a neighbour recommendation. They're on their phone or laptop, mildly skeptical, and looking for trust signals before booking. The chore disgusts them — they want someone else to own it permanently, not just this once. They're not price-sensitive at $20/month; they're confidence-sensitive.

Secondary audience: rental property managers who service multiple properties and want a recurring, set-and-forget solution.

## Product Purpose

The Bin Boy is a local mobile bin cleaning service. Technicians come to the customer's home, power wash garbage bins at 200° with odour-neutralizing solution, and return them to the garage. No effort required from the homeowner.

The landing page has one job: convert a skeptical visitor into a booked appointment or a phone call. Success is a completed contact form or a tap on the phone number. Every section exists to remove a reason not to book.

## Brand Personality

Confident, clean, professional. A local operator who earns trust through competence, not charm. Friendly but not chatty. Precise without being corporate. The name has personality baked in — lean into it subtly, never become silly.

## Anti-references

- Cheap local business sites: stock photography, cluttered layouts, no visual hierarchy, low-effort typography. The design should look more considered than a typical tradie site.
- Generic SaaS landing pages: floating mockups, "Trusted by 10,000+ teams", gradient hero with a hero-metric dashboard screenshot.
- Corporate cleaning brands: sanitised stock photos of uniformed maids, zero personality, feels like a franchise.

## Design Principles

1. **Trust through specifics.** Every claim earns its place with a concrete detail: 200°, fully insured, bins returned to garage, text when we're done. No vague adjectives like "amazing" or "best." Proof beats assertion.
2. **Local before large.** This is a neighbourhood business, not a franchise. The design should feel like it was made for this community — real people, real service area, a real phone number you can actually call.
3. **Clarity converts.** The visitor's only job is to decide whether to book. Every element that doesn't help that decision is a liability. Cut visual noise ruthlessly.
4. **Clean reflects the product.** The interface should feel as clean as the bins the service promises. No clutter, no competing visual priorities, no unnecessary decoration.
5. **Competence, not cheerleading.** Confidence through precision. Skip the superlatives; let the specifics and social proof speak. The brand doesn't need to tell you it's great — the 200° stat and the five-star reviews do that.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA compliance. Good contrast ratios, keyboard navigable, screen reader friendly. Support prefers-reduced-motion for the floating logo animation and any other motion effects.
